Frequently asked questions

Quick answers about the Prayer City movement, volunteering in person or online, groups, and what happens next.

What is the Houston World Cup Prayer City movement?

We are raising prayer volunteers and setting up prayer tents in the NRG Stadium area as we welcome visitors during the World Cup. We expect well over half a million guests in Houston—and we want them to encounter Jesus, many born again and saved, carrying the gospel back to their home countries.

It’s a unique moment: nations are coming to us. We can’t travel to every country at once—but when they come here, we can lead them to Christ and help them take the good news home to share with others.

I’ll be away during the summer—can I still volunteer?

Yes. That’s why we have Social Media & Virtual Support: you can help from anywhere in the world by reposting, sharing, replying, and engaging with posts online so more people hear the message.

You can also participate in prayer alongside the movement virtually when you’re not in Houston—your intercession still matters. Signed-in volunteers get daily social posts on the dashboard to share.

Can friends, family, or my church group get involved?

Yes. Please share with friends, family, your church, Bible study, or prayer group. Many of you can sign up and serve together.

What if I’m afraid or nervous to serve?

You don’t need to be afraid. We held in-person training — if you missed it, join our Thursday, June 11 virtual session (6pm CT) on Zoom. You’ll have clear coordination, morning prayer at the tent, and Tricia Hill (Prayer City Coordinator) at check-in. Your volunteer dashboard and volunteer hub connect you with the team so you’re not alone.

Can I sign up for multiple days and time slots?

Yes. On the sign-up form you can choose several dates and multiple time slots—pick everything that fits your schedule.

Call Tricia Hill at 832-277-3831 if you need help finding the tent on serve day.

Volunteer hours: Most serve days run 11:00 AM – 3:00 PM (hourly slots). Friday, June 26 is an evening shift: 6:00 PM – 10:00 PM.

What happens when I arrive on my serve day?
  1. Park on the street near 1325 La Concha Lane, Houston, TX (free street parking near the tents).
  2. Walk to the prayer tents and check in with Tricia Hill, Prayer City Coordinator — receive your T-shirt, volunteer tags, and a short briefing.
  3. Join worship and prayer at the tent; Tricia will pray with you if your shift starts later.
  4. Get materials and serve in your assigned area (prayer, evangelism, or your role).

Arrive about 15–30 minutes before your shift when you can. Tricia Hill’s day-of phone number is listed on your dashboard and volunteer hub after you sign in — use it if you’re lost, running late, or need help on serve day.

When will I know my assignment and the address?

Your tent assignment (Tent 1–7) appears on your volunteer dashboard — confirm at check-in with Tricia Hill.

How do I get my volunteer T-shirt?

We are printing Houston Prayer City shirts for volunteers. After you sign in, open your dashboard and save your size (S, M, L, or X) — or email ddbs.htx@gmail.com.

You’ll receive your shirt when you check in on your serve day with the Prayer City Coordinator.

Optional: if you’re able to donate toward shirt costs, gifts are welcome via Zeffy (100% to the movement).

What should I wear and bring?

Wear: comfortable clothes and walking shoes suitable for Houston summer heat.

Bring: water, sunscreen, a charged phone, and anything personal you need for a long day outdoors. Your Prayer City T-shirt and volunteer tag are provided at check-in.

We’re coming as a group—can we share the same tent?

Yes. We’ll do our best to place your group together. Send a group request by emailing ddbs.htx@gmail.com with your names, emails, and preferred dates—so we can assign you to the same tent when possible.

What happens after the World Cup prayer outreach?

This movement is part of a longer story. We have more programs and events planned beyond the tournament.

View upcoming programs & key dates — on the sign-up page. After you’re signed in, you’ll also see highlights on your volunteer dashboard.
